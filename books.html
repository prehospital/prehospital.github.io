Books

Useful books to further your knowledge. Considerable savings can be made if you opt to purchase an older edition or second hand book.

Tweet

Anatomy and Physiology

  • Principles of Anatomy and Physiology (volume 1 & 2)

    Gerard J. Tortora and Bryan H. Derrickson. John Wiley's and Sons incorporated.

    Detailed book on anatomy and physiology of the human body.

    Google "Tortora Principles of Anatomy and Physiology"

  • Ross and Wilson Anatomy and Physiology in Health and Disease

    Anne Waugh and ‎Allison Grant. Elsevier Health Sciences.

    Arguably more approachable than Tortora's Princibles of Anatomy and Physiology, however less detailed in areas.

    Google "Ross and Wilson Anatomy and Physiology in Health and Disease"

Pharmacology

  • British National Formulary (BNF)

    Joint Formulary Committee.

    Drug reference book. Easily fits in your bag.

    www.bnf.org

  • Rang & Dale's Pharmacology

    Rang et al. Elsevier Churchill Livingstone.

    General knowledge book on the topic of different classes of drugs and their effect.

    Google "Rang & Dale's Pharmacology"

Guidelines and clinical practice

  • UK Ambulance Service Clinical Practice Guidelines 2016

    AACE and JRCALC. Class Professional Publishing.

    Official guidelines for paramedic practice in the UK. Offers guidance on various patient presentations and the usage of drugs available to UK paramedics. Also features a quick reference section of drug dosages for different children ages.

    Available as a large reference edition, pocket book or mobile app.

    aace.org.uk/clinical-practice-guidelines

  • Oxford Handbook of Clinical Examination and Practical Skills

    James Thomas and Tanya Monaghan. Oxford University Press.

    Dense pocket book on conducting various clinical examinations. Organised by body systems (e.g. Cardiovascular, Respiratory, Neurological etc.).

    Book page at Oxford University Press

  • Oxford Handbook of Clinical Medicine

    Longmore et al. Oxford University Press.

    Dense pocket book aimed at junior and foundation doctors. Offers various clinical advice. Very suitable for paramedic knowledge.

    Book page at Oxford University Press
